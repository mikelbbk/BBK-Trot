How This Plan Works

  • Wednesday is the key quality day each week — intervals, tempo, or MP blocks. Friday (7 km technical) and Saturday (long run) are the other two fixed running days.
  • Monday and Sunday are full rest every week — no exceptions. At sub-3 training loads, these recovery days are non-negotiable.
  • Tuesday and Thursday always have outdoor strength at 6 am. On some weeks a PM run follows — these optional extra sessions are marked in the plan and add meaningful volume in key weeks.
  • Friday is technical — form drills, strides, controlled fartlek or MP bursts within the 7 km. Never turn Friday into a tempo — the long run follows the next day.
  • Long runs alternate big / step-back every week. Even at this level, the step-back weeks are not optional — they're where adaptation happens.
  • Sub-3 requires near-perfect race execution. Practise nutrition every long run over 90 min from Week 8 — same gel brand and timing you'll use on race day.
  • Optional tune-up half marathon: Week 7 (step-back week). Race it at ~1:27–1:29 to test fitness.
  • Taper madness hits harder at this training volume. Weeks 15–17 will feel terrible. Your fitness is locked in — protect it, don't chase it.

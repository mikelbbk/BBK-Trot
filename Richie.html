How This Plan Works

  • Wednesday is the key quality day each week — intervals, tempo, or MP blocks. Friday (7 km technical) and Saturday (long run) are the other two fixed running days.
  • Monday and Sunday are full rest every week — no exceptions. These two recovery days are as important as any run.
  • Tuesday and Thursday always have outdoor strength at 6 am. On some weeks an afternoon run follows — these optional sessions are marked in the plan. Never skip strength to protect a PM run.
  • Friday is technical — form drills, strides, or light fartlek within the 7 km. It is deliberately short so Saturday's long run gets fresh legs. Don't push Friday.
  • Long runs alternate big / step-back every week. A big Saturday is always followed by a lighter one. No two hard long runs back-to-back.
  • From Week 8 onwards, practise race nutrition (gels/chews) on every long run over 90 min. Use the exact product you'll race with.
  • Optional tune-up half marathon: Week 7 (step-back week, long run is only 14 km). A great fitness check at this stage.
  • Taper madness is real — Weeks 15–17 you will feel sluggish and restless. That is adaptation. Trust it.
